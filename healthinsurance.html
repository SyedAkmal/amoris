Health Insurance

Health insurance is a way to pay for health care. It protects you from paying the full costs of medical services when youâ€™re injured or sick. Just like car insurance or home insurance, you choose a plan and agree to pay a certain rate, or premium, each month. In return, your health insurer agrees to pay a portion of your covered medical costs. Payments by your health insurance are typically based on discounts they negotiate with doctors and hospitals.

Types Of Health Plans
1. Health maintenance organizations (HMOs)
2. Preferred provider organizations (PPOs)
3. Exclusive provider organizations (EPOs)
4. Point-of-service (POS) plans.
5. High-deductible health plans (HDHPs), which may be linked to health savings accounts (HSAs)
Eligibility of health insurance
The minimum age from which a person can start to apply a health insurance is 18 years. As one grows older on the basis of medical background, coverage cost increases.

Documents required


1. Proof of Age
You will have to provide the age proof of all the persons who will be getting covered with the health insurance policy opted by you. Following are the acceptable documents you can provide for this:
a) Birth certificate
b) 10th or 12th mark sheet
c) Passport
d) Aadhaar card
e) Voting ID
f) Driving license
g) PAN card etc.
2. Identity Proof
You will be required to produce any of the following
a) Aadhaar card
b) Passport
c) Voting ID
d) Driving license
e) PAN card
3. Address Proof
You will be required to submit your permanent proof of address. You can submit any of the following documents for the same:
a) Electricity bill
b) Telephone bill
c) Ration card
d) Passport
e) Aadhaar card
f) Driving license
g) Voting ID
h) Passport size photos

4. Sometimes health insurance policies might require you to undergo a few medical tests. You will be required to submit the medical reports detailing the results of these tests as well.
